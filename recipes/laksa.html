Laksa

Description

Laksa is a popular Southeast Asian noodle soup with a rich and creamy coconut milk base, flavored with spicy curry and aromatic herbs. Originating from Malaysia and Singapore, it combines a variety of textures and flavors, making it a delicious and comforting dish. There are many variations of laksa, but the most common version includes ingredients like rice noodles, shrimp, chicken, and bean sprouts.

Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g cooked chicken, shredded
  • 150g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons laksa paste
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 lime wedges (for serving)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the laksa paste and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Stir in the coconut milk and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the shredded chicken and shrimp to the pot. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and the chicken is heated through.
  5. Season the soup with salt to taste.
  6. To serve, divide the cooked noodles among bowls. Ladle the laksa soup over the noodles.
  7. Top with bean sprouts and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve with lime wedges on the side.
